1. The Story We're Telling

WrenchIQ helps shops get their most important jobs done better — powered by Predii's automotive intelligence. GWG sets the targets. Shop owners see how to hit them. Service writers get decision-by-decision guidance at the counter. Every action still happens in the SMS. WrenchIQ proves the impact.

The demo tells this story in three acts:

1

Targets — GWG Sets the Objectives

Taylor Mitchell at GWG configures WrenchIQ with specific operational objectives tied to the 4 jobs. Not abstract KPI thresholds but real business directives: "Increase oil change ARO by $75 and prioritize Brand X oil." "All brake ROs must have complete 3C narratives with TSB references." She can see red/yellow/green which locations are following them.

2

Guidance — The Agent Works Alongside the SMS

Protractor is the primary workspace. WrenchIQ's agent panel sits alongside. As the service writer works, the agent provides intelligence at each decision — diagnosis support, 3C quality scoring, data-backed upsell. Then the shop owner sees operational intelligence: how their shop tracks against GWG's objectives, with Predii-powered root-cause analysis.

3

Proof — Impact Dashboard

What the agent recommended, what was acted on, and the measurable P&L result. This is how GWG justifies per-rooftop spend and how Predii proves the intelligence layer's value.

2. The Overlay Principle

WrenchIQ is NOT a shop management system. It is an AI intelligence overlay that sits alongside the shop's existing SMS (Protractor, Vehlo, Mitchell1, etc.). It consumes data from the SMS and third-party sources, runs it through Predii's intelligence engine, and surfaces recommendations. All actions are performed in the SMS, not in WrenchIQ.

WrenchIQ owns: the agent panel (contextual recommendations), operational intelligence (shop owner), GWG objectives + location tracking (corporate), and impact dashboard (proof).

The SMS owns: RO queue, estimate builder, scheduling, parts ordering, customer portal, DVI, billing — all workflow execution.

3. Why These Jobs

There are dozens of things an AI agent could do in a shop. We picked these 4 because each one passes three filters:

Filter 1: Requires Predii's Automotive Intelligence. A generic AI agent with SMS data access couldn't do it. The job depends on Predii's knowledge graph — the ontology mapping symptoms to DTCs to root causes to repairs to parts, trained on millions of repair orders. If someone could replicate it with GPT + an SMS API connector, it doesn't make the cut.

Filter 2: Connects Directly to P&L. The value must be measurable in dollars — incremental revenue, cost avoidance, margin improvement, or risk reduction.

Filter 3: Works as an Overlay. The job can be done through recommendations that the service writer or shop owner acts on in the SMS. No WrenchIQ-owned workflow required.

Other jobs could be added later — and pricing could scale by job/capability. These 4 are the starting set because they're the clearest intersection of Predii's moat, measurable value, and overlay-friendly delivery.

4. The 4 Jobs to Get Done

Benchmarks: $1.2M annual revenue, 6 bays, ~2,400 ROs/year, $500 avg RO, 50% gross margin.

1
Intelligent Intake & Diagnosis
SERVICE WRITER

Customer says "weird sound driving 80mph." WrenchIQ's agent panel shows probable DTC, relevant TSB, estimated labor/parts — before the car goes on the lift. The service writer uses this to write a better RO and ask the right diagnostic questions in Protractor.

WHY PREDII Predii's ontology maps natural language complaints → DTCs → root causes for specific VIN/make/model/mileage from millions of historical ROs. A generic LLM can summarize, but it can't diagnose.
VALUE PER SHOP / MONTH $2,000–$4,000. Fewer comebacks ($500–800 each), faster diagnosis = more throughput per bay.
GWG OBJECTIVE "First-visit diagnosis accuracy > 90%. Flag locations with comeback rate above 5%."
2
RO Quality & Compliance (3C Narrative)
SERVICE WRITER

Tech writes "catalytic converter broken / fix catalytic converter" in Protractor. WrenchIQ flags it as incomplete, generates a proper 3C narrative with TSB references, and scores it. The service writer copies the improved narrative into the SMS.

WHY PREDII Predii's RO Story Writer understands automotive repair terminology, complaint/cause/correction relationships, and what constitutes a defensible narrative. Trained on millions of ROs.
VALUE PER SHOP / MONTH $1,000–$3,000. Fewer warranty rejections ($400–1,200 each), reduced liability exposure.
GWG OBJECTIVE "All brake and diagnostic ROs must score 80%+ on 3C completeness with TSB references."
3
Smart Upsell at the Counter
SERVICE WRITER

2018 Subaru Outback at 92K miles comes in for an oil change. Agent panel says: "Head gasket inspection at 95K — 71% of similar Outbacks had this done within 5K miles. Cabin filter last replaced 34K ago." Data-backed, vehicle-specific recommendations the service writer can present with confidence.

WHY PREDII Predii knows repair patterns by make/model/year/mileage from the shop's RO history and GWG's network data. Vehicle-specific, not generic maintenance schedules.
VALUE PER SHOP / MONTH $3,000–$8,000. +$30–50 ARO on 25% of eligible ROs = $3K–5K incremental revenue.
GWG OBJECTIVE "Increase oil change ARO by $75. Prioritize Brand X oil. Target 30% upsell acceptance."
4
Operational Intelligence
SHOP OWNER

Shop owner sees how their shop tracks against GWG's objectives. Not just metrics — Predii explains why in automotive terms: "Approval rate on brake jobs dropped 12%. Root cause: 3C narratives averaging 54% completeness — customers don't trust the diagnosis. Top GWG locations average 82%+."

WHY PREDII Predii traces P&L outcomes to root causes through the repair data — connecting margin problems to specific repair categories, narrative quality, tech patterns. A BI dashboard shows the number; Predii tells you why.
VALUE PER SHOP / MONTH $2,000–$5,000. Drives adoption of Jobs 1–3. One fixed margin leak or approval drag per month compounds across the business.
GWG OBJECTIVE "Weekly performance brief for every shop owner. Flag locations with approval rate below 75% — provide root-cause analysis."

Combined value per shop: $8,000–$20,000/month. At 100+ locations, even the conservative end makes per-rooftop pricing of a few hundred dollars/month a straightforward ROI conversation.
